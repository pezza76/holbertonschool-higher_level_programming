In the absence of the Mayor, the Chief Executive Officer declared the Council Meeting open
at 6.30pm and advised members of the public gallery that the meeting is being recorded and
streamed live on the internet to enhance the accessibility of Council meetings to the broader
Bayside community.
The Chief Executive Officer called for a motion for the Deputy Mayor to take the Chair for this
evening’s Council Meeting.
Moved: Cr Evans Seconded: Cr Castelli
That the Deputy Mayor, Cr Samuel-King take the chair for the entire Council Meeting.
CARRIED

Cr Martin read the prayer at the commencement of the meeting.
O God
Bless this City, Bayside,
Give us courage, strength and wisdom,
So that our deliberations,
May be for the good of all,
Amen
2. Acknowledgement of Country
Cr Castelli read the acknowledgement of Country.
 Bayside City Council proudly acknowledges the Bunurong People of the Kulin
Nation as the Traditional Owners and Custodians of this land, and we pay our
respects to their Elders, past, present and emerging as well as any Aboriginal or
Torres Strait Islander community members with us today.
 Council acknowledges the Bunurong’s continuing relationship to the land and
waterways and respects that their connection and spiritual identity is maintained
through ancient ceremonies, songlines, dance, art and living culture.
 Council pays tribute to the invaluable contributions of the Bunurong and other
Aboriginal and Torres Strait Island Elders who have guided and continue to guide
the work we do.

6. Public Question Time
1. Pastor Bruce Corben
Pastor Bruce Corben submitted a question regarding item 10.16 on tonight’s Agenda, the
Level Crossing Removal Project Announcement, Highett, and Pastor Corben asked:
Question
What does Bayside Council and Bayside Council staff including the CEO know regarding this
proposed acquisition of Grace Heart Community Church and the land in the Lyle Anderson
Reserve, the back of the Highett Bowling Club and the back of the Fox Dunkley Public
Housing Estate next to the railway reserve and the impact of the proposed Highett Road
Level Crossing removal and new Highett Station on this land?
Response from the CEO
Beyond the information contained in the announcement for this project as presented in item
10.6 on tonight’s Agenda, Council officers have been involved in one preliminary meeting
with LXRP. Land acquisitions required for the delivery of this project were not discussed in
this meeting. Council officers are not aware of specific parcels of land identified for the
purposes of acquisition.
2. Pastor Bruce Corben
Pastor Bruce Corben submitted a further question regarding item 10.16 on tonight’s Agenda,
the Level Crossing Removal Project Announcement, Highett, and Pastor Corben asked:
Question
Have the Bayside Council, CEO and/or staff in any way indicated in their discussions with the
Level Crossing Removal Authority that they will support the relocation of the Highett Train
Station from its current location to be nearer the area of the Lyle Anderson Reserve despite
the current Bayside Council Highett Structure Plan stating that the Highett Station should
remain in its current location?
Response from the CEO
Council officers are not aware of any proposed land acquisitions required for the delivery of
the project at this stage. No discussions in relation to the relocation of Highett Train Station
have occurred between Council officers and the Level Crossing Removal Project.

10.6 FUTURE USE OF MELROSE STREET, SANDRINGHAM
Environment, Recreation and Infrastructure - Climate, Waste and Integrated Transport
File No: PSF/22/26 – Doc No: DOC/22/368743
It is recorded that Mr Eric Purcell, Mr Ilias Degrenis, and Mr Simon Rankin (on behalf of
Sandringham Traders Association Committee) each submitted a written statement in
relation to this item.
It is recorded that Mr Andrew Watson (on behalf of Melrose Street Traders), Mr Chris
Voce, Mr Ray Purcell, and Dr Vicki Karalis (on behalf of Sandringham Foreshore
Association) each spoke for 2 minutes in relation to this item.
Moved: Cr Stitfold Seconded: Cr Evans OAM
That Council:
1. notes the report and the outcome of community consultation and the traffic impact
assessment
2. initiates the statutory process in accordance with Schedule 11, Clause 10(1)(c) of
the Local Government Act 1989 and undertakes public engagement, including
writing to business and property owners of Sandringham Village, in accordance with
Section 223 of the Act for a temporary road closure of part Melrose Street,
Sandringham commencing in November 2023 for a minimum period of four months
3. considers a report from the Head, Transport for Victoria prior to the trial closure
4. undertakes targeted engagement with key stakeholders to assist in the development
of a concept design for the space during the trial closure
5. refers the allocation of $100,000 to 2023–24 budget process which would support
the closure ($40,000) and activation ($60,000) of Melrose Street, Sandringham
during any trial, should it be supported
6. refers the allocation of $95,000 to 2023–24 budget process to support the
introduction of parking sensors and dynamic signage, and updates the timed parking
restrictions to optimise utilisation and turnover of the carpark located at 122 Beach
Road, Sandringham, should the changes be supported
7. receives a report at a future Council meeting in 2023 detailing the outcomes of the
